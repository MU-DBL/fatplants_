General Background

The galactolipids monogalactosyldiacylglycerol (MGDG) and digalactosyldiacylglycerol (DGDG) are prevalent membrane lipids in organisms that carry out oxygenic photosynthesis, such as land plants, algae, and cyanobacteria [Doermann13]. MGDG and DGDG constitute about 50% and 30%, respectively, of the total lipids in the majority of photosynthetic membranes.

Crystallographic analysis of photosystem complexes revealed that stoichiometric amounts of both types of galactolipids are bound in the interior of photosystem complexes [Jones07, Umena11], and it has been shown that a galactolipid-deficient mutant of Arabidopsis thaliana lacks photosynthetic activity [Kobayashi07].

About This Pathway

The biosynthesis of galactolipids in plants is localized to the envelope membranes of chloroplasts. Thus both MGDG and DGDG have to be transported to the thylakoid membranes during chloroplast development.

The glycosylglycerides are synthesized using diacylglycerol (DAG) as the starting compound. The first enzyme of galactolipid synthesis, EC 2.4.1.46, monogalactosyldiacylglycerol synthase, was isolated from cucumber seedlings [Shimojima97]. This enzyme transfers a galactose residue from UDP-α-D-galactose onto diacylglycerol. The enzyme inverts the anomeric configuration of the galactose, resulting in a β bond to DAG. Three genes encoding MGDG synthases were isolated from Arabidopsis [Awai01]. However, only MGD1 is crucial for the synthesis of most of the MGDG lipid required for thylakoid membrane assembly in chloroplasts. MGD2 and MGD3 are only expressed in certain plant organs or under specific conditions such as phosphate limitation [Kobayashi09].

The second enzyme involved is EC 2.4.1.241, digalactosyldiacylglycerol synthase. Arabidopsis contains two genes encoding the enzyme - DGD1 and DGD2 [Dormann99]. Both are UDP-galactose-dependent galactosyltransferases that transfer galactose from UDP-α-D-galactose onto MGDG with retention of the anomeric configuration of the sugar moiety.

In addition to MGDG and DGDG, plants contain low amounts of oligogalactolipids with three or four galactose residues in their head groups. The synthesis of these oligogalactolipids is independent of DGDG synthases DGD1 and DGD2, and is carried out by EC 2.4.1.184, galactolipid galactosyltransferase (GGGT) [Xu03], which is encoded by the SFR2 gene [Moellering10]. GGGT is not related to glycosyltransferases and belongs to the family of glycosylhydrolases. The enzyme acts on MGDG, converting it into di-, tri-, and tetragalactosyldiacylglycerol during low temperature stress. The galactosyl linkages are all in β form [Xu03]. The GGGT activity provides an important mechanism for the stabilization of the chloroplast membranes during freezing and drought stress. SFR2 is essential for the adaptation of Arabidopsis to freezing stress [Thorlby04, Fourrier08]
