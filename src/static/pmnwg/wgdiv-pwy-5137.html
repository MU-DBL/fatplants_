Supporting evidence for this pathway in Aegilops tauschii: This pathway is on the list of Ubiquitous Plant Pathways (UPP) that was generated for the COMET project, which includes pathways that are thought to be present in all or most land plants. All UPP pathways are automatically imported into databases regardless of Pathologic predictions.

Summary from MetaCyc:

When fatty acids that contain a cis-double bond on odd-numbered carbons are processed by short-chain acyl-CoA oxidase (see fatty acid β-oxidation II (plant peroxisome)), they are converted to a (3Z)-3-enoyl-CoA. These compounds must be converted to a (2E)-2-enoyl-CoA before they can be processed further [Graham02b].

Monofunctional proteins having such activity have been described from many organisms, including mammals, plants and bacteria.

In plants, this activity is also a part of of the multifunction protein (MFP) (see multifunctional protein MFP I, multifunctional protein MFP II, multifunctional protein MFP III).
