Summary from MetaCyc:

Some of the most prevalent fatty acids in lipid storage tissues, such as linoleate and α-linolenate, contain cis-double bonds on even-numbered carbons. When such fatty acids are processed by the first enzyme of the core β-oxidation pathway, short-chain acyl-CoA oxidase (see fatty acid β-oxidation II (plant peroxisome)), they are converted into a (2E,4Z)-2,4-dienoyl-CoA. This intermediate cannot proceed directly through the core β-oxidation pathway, and must be processed via either of two alternative routes.

In the first route a (2E,4Z)-2,4-dienoyl-CoA enters the so called reductase/isomerase pathway, which involves the enzymes 2,4-dienoyl-CoA reductase and delta-3, delta2-enoyl-CoA isomerase. In this route the dienoyl-CoA is converted to a (2E)-2-enoyl-CoA, which can be processed further by the core β-oxidation pathway.

Alternatively, a (2E,4Z)-2,4-dienoyl-CoA can be converted to a (2Z)-2-enoyl-CoA through one more cycle of core beta-oxidation pathway. A cis-2-enoyl-CoA cannot be completely degraded via the core β-oxidation pathway since its hydratation yields a 2,3,4-saturated (3R)-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA which is not a substrate for (S)-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA:NAD+ oxidoreductase. Thus, the D-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA enters the so called epimerase pathway that involves converting a 2,3,4-saturated (3R)-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA to a 2,3,4-saturated (3S)-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA by 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA epimerase. Alternatively, this conversion can be performed by two stereo-specific enoyl-CoA hydratases. The L-3-hydroxyacyl-CoA then returns to the core β-oxidation pathway for complete degradation.

The enoyl-CoA isomerase and 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA epimerase activities, together with two enzyme activities also found in the core β-oxidation pathway, namely 2-enoyl-CoA hydratase and 3-hydroxyacyl-CoA dehydrogenase, are attributed to a single multifunctional protein (MFP).

Studies in Cucumis sativus cotyledon suggested the epimerase pathway is much more effective than the reductase/isomerase pathway [Engeland91a], whereas studies in Arabidopsis thaliana col indicated that both pathways have a similar contribution [Allenbach00].
