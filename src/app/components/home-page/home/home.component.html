Introduction

Fatplants is a web portal with analysis and visualization tools for plant fat-related genes, proteins and metabolism. It empowers researchers to develop and test hypotheses, and design crops with value-added compositions. In particular, collating data in Fatplants on oilseed engineering can accelerate the process of knowledge discovery and crop design for biology researchers with little computer skills.

As a “one-stop-shop” community web resource, it covers broad data of Glycine max, Arabidopsis thaliana, Camelina sativa, Cuphea, and Pennycress generated from our studies, curated from public sources, and collected from the literature to help understand metabolism synthesis pathways and related protein interactions. Some data and annotations come from Uniprot, TAIR, LIPIDMAPS, OPSIN, LMPD, PlantFAdb and ARALIPS. FatPlants also includes gene and protein information cards, Blast search function, and structure visualization.

Site Features

Data
  • Integrated data of fatty acid-related proteins, including genes for Arabidopsis, Soybean, Camellia, Cuphea, and Pennycress.
  • Functional information for each protein, including post-translation modification sites and functional structure regions.
  • Physicochemical information of fatty acids.
Analysis
  • Sequence similarity search based on Blastp against known fatty-acid related proteins.
  • 3D-structure similarity search and visualization using in-house G2S against the fatty-acid related proteins.
  • Pathway mapping to KEGG and Reactome.
  • Collection of fatty-acid related pathway pictures from the literature and interactive visualization using our in-house machine learning image understanding tool.
Visualization
  • Visualization of protein-protein interaction for fatty-related proteins.
  • Visualization of GO Enrichment network for fatty acid related proteins.
